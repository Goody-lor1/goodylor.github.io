Introduction

The first thing people can think of percussion should be DRUMS, right? Actually, percussion include many different kinds of instruments. For example, shaker, bells and agogo bell.

Percussion is the backbone or a base of a band. It provides a beat to other instruments to follow. From band to orchestra, percussion also acts a really important character.

The most basic and original percussion instrument is our hands. When we are clapping our hands, we make sounds for beats with such an easy action. Some people also use things in daily life to make sounds and jam music together. Therefore, people can involve the beat and enjoy the music easily.

Percussion Instrument

Below are the instrumrnts that I have played

Xylophone

  • Consists of wooden bar
  • Played with very hard rubber mallets or wrapped mallets
  • Crisp sound and high range
  • The arrangement of the wood is similar to piano keyboard

    • Marimba

  • Similar to xylophone
  • Played with wrapped mallets
  • Round sound and wider range
  • The longer the resonance tube, the deeper the sound

    • Bells

  • Consists of metal plates
  • Played with very hard mallets which are made of plastic or metal
  • Limited to the upper register

    • Vibraphone

  • Consists of metal plates
  • Played with soft mallets
  • Accompanied by a pedal, will not stopped by the sound pad so that the sound can be longer

    • Snare drum

  • Composed of a number of fine spiral metal wires
  • It can be locked(close to the lower drum surface) or released (leave the lower drum surface)
  • When the wires are locked, the sound is penetrating
  • When the wires are released, the sound will be turned into a sound to “Tom Tom”

    • Agogo bell

  • Yoruba (a language spoken in West Africa): agogo, meaning bell
  • origins in traditional Yoruba music
  • One of the oldest Samba instrument
  • There are usually two bells nowadays
  • One sounds ‘ding’, one sounds ‘dong’

    Percussion Mallet

    Different instrument played with different kinds of mallet

    Why we need so many different mallets and sticks? Do the colour of them means something? These two questions are always questioned by a beginner of playing the percussion. Because different instruments or even different materials with the same kinds of instrument need different mallet and sticks.

    Wrapped Mallets

    Marimba, Vibraphone

    Unwrapped Mallets

    Xylophone, Bells

    Drum Sticks

    Drums
    Wrapped Mallets

    Wrapped mallets have various thickness. The thicker the mallet, the lower range of mallet percussion should play. Because more thread is wrapped on the mallet, it will be thicker and protected the materials of the mallet percussion, such as the wood of xylophone. Also, it can be more powerful when people hit the materials.

    Unwrapped mallet

    Unwrapped mallets have various elasticity through different materials. In picture, the yellow one is less elastic. The tiffany blue one is more elastic. Usually, the size and materials of ‘ball’ defines the output of the sound.

    Drum sticks

    Drum sticks have distinct types. The major type of tip named round and arrow. In picture, it is round one. Personally, I think that round one is easier to catch up.

    Story between Me and Percussion

    From 2008 to 2019

    The first in-touch of percussion is in my primary school. I played as an extracurricular activity when I first get in touch with it. In the same period of time, I also played the clarinet. Therefore, both of them can train my sensitive to music. After that, I joined the school percussion team. It is different from the orchestra. The whole team was just playing percussion instruments. In those 3 years in the school team, let me fall in love with percussion. I can get satisfaction from the teachers and also from the teammates. The team was just like a family. We will help each other within the team whatever carry the instrument or finding the beat of sheet music. It is one of the reasons that I love to play the percussion. I love teamwork.

    After primary school, I also tried to learn percussion in my secondary school. However, my secondary school do not have a percussion band. The music teacher recommended me to try to play percussion in the orchestra. But I do not like the cooperation in the orchestra so that I quit the school orchestra after secondary one. That’s why I do not play the percussion for about 5 years.

    In Oct 2018, my teacher asked me to do you want to play the percussion again. Therefore, I play the percussion again.
